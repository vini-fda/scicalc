Scicalc

This is a scientific calculator that handles inputs with uncertainties. If you have a measurement of, say, (100 ± 13) mL of water, and you add (20 ± 3) mL to it, what would be the result, given in the same notation?

Well, what are you waiting for? try to evaluate it in the input field below!

Try it out!

Features

Currently, this calculator supports:

  • Basic floating-point numbers(AKA 'real numbers'), such as 23.0, 42, -.033
  • The numeric constants e("Euler's number") and π(greek letter "pi")
  • Quantities with uncertatinties of the form x ± y, such as 23.0 ± 1.0 and -1.000 ± 0.033
  • Basic arithmetic operations, i.e. addition, subtraction, multiplication and division
  • Parenthesized and nested expressions, such as ((42 ± 3) / (1 ± 0.1)) * 2.0 - π * e

Remarks

Note that some measurements may not make any sense at all, due to their inaccuracies.
Quantities such as 0.1 ± 13 are very inaccurate(the error is two orders of magnitude bigger than the mean value!), therefore(due to error propagation), the results of any expressions involving these quantities will be even more innacurate.
